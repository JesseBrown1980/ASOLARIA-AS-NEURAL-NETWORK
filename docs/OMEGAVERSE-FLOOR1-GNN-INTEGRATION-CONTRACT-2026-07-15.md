The trained Omega omnibit and Omega GNN are coupled but distinct. The omnibit is the trained

## Improvement acceptance

For parent `P` and child `C`, the held-out set and evaluator bytes must be committed before
`C` is generated. The same held-out set evaluates both bodies. Promotion requires:

```text
score(C) - score(P) > 0
confidence lower bound of the delta > measured noise upper bound
leakage audit = PASS_NO_OVERLAP
reverse replay = PASS
rollback state = PRESENT
```

Stochastic claims also bind seeds, repeated trials, evaluator version, confidence method, and
integer or exact-rational score representation. More training, a larger catalog, or a changed
evaluator is not by itself improvement.

## Cube-native glyph languages

English is an operator-facing translation layer, not the assumed native representation. A
cube-language claim must bind its cube PID, vocabulary, grammar, semantic relations, held-out
composition set, forward translator, and reverse translator. Promotion separately tests:

- more than symbol substitution;
- novel composition;
- native-to-Omega-to-native reversibility;
- cross-cube meaning preservation;
- retention of the native language after alignment.

A witness PID proves identity and lineage. It does not substitute for a semantic evaluation.

The resulting control law is:

```text
FEDENV validate and cosign
  -> resolve PID, slot, or Brown-Hilbert coordinate
  -> choose priority lane and room
```

Room identity is persistent; an emitter is a temporarily energized function. The dispatcher can
therefore address a large room space without materializing every room at once. The Omega/OmniGNN
layer selects and relates typed rooms; it does not replace dispatcher authority, queueing, or
backpressure.
